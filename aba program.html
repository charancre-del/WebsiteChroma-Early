Clinical Services

Modern ABA:
Science with Heart.

Applied Behavior Analysis is the most effective evidence-based treatment for autism. We focus on teaching skills that matter—communication, independence, and joy—without compromising your child's personality.

How It Works Book Consultation

Skill Acquisition

Focus Areas

ABA is about much more than reducing behavior. It is about expanding your child's world through skill building.

Communication

Functional communication training (FCT) to replace frustration with words, signs, or AAC.

Social Skills

Joint attention, turn-taking, and navigating peer interactions in our mock classroom settings.

Independence

Toileting, feeding, dressing, and following daily routines to foster self-confidence.

School Readiness

Sitting for circle time, following group instructions, and transitioning between activities.

The Process

How We Build Your Child's Program

1

Assessment & Intake

Our BCBA conducts a comprehensive skills assessment (VB-MAPP or ABLLS-R) to identify strengths and barriers. We listen to your family's priorities first.

2

Custom Treatment Plan

We create a tailored roadmap with measurable goals. This plan is submitted to insurance for authorization (we handle the paperwork).

3

1:1 Therapy Begins

Your child is paired with a Registered Behavior Technician (RBT). We start with "pairing"—playing and building trust—before placing demands.

4

Data & Generalization

We track data daily. As skills are mastered in the clinic, we work on generalizing them to home and school environments.

Common Questions About ABA

How many hours will my child need?

It varies by medical necessity. Focused programs (targeting specific behaviors) are typically 10-20 hours/week, while comprehensive early intervention programs are often 25-35 hours/week to maximize neuroplasticity.

Is ABA "training" my child like a robot?

Absolutely not. That is old-school ABA. Our modern, assent-based approach focuses on functional skills and personality. We want your child to be their most independent self, not someone else.

Do you involve parents?

Yes! Parent training is a required component of our program. We empower you with the strategies we use in the clinic so you can support your child at home.
