Focus Areas

Speech therapy addresses the full spectrum of communication, from the mechanics of sound to the nuances of conversation.

Articulation

Improving the clarity of speech sounds so your child can be understood by peers and family.

Language (Expressive/Receptive)

Building vocabulary, sentence structure, and the ability to understand and follow directions.

Social Pragmatics

Understanding the "rules" of conversation: turn-taking, staying on topic, and reading body language.

AAC Devices

Expertise in Augmentative and Alternative Communication for non-vocal children.

The Process

More Than Just Words

1

Evaluation

We use standardized assessments (like the CELF-P2 or PLS-5) alongside play-based observation to understand your child's communication profile.

2

Personalized Goals

Whether it's saying "mama" for the first time or navigating a complex social conflict, we set functional goals that matter to your family.

3

Play-Based Therapy

We follow the child's lead. We use bubbles to teach "pop," cars to teach "go," and games to teach turn-taking.

4

Parent Empowerment

We teach you strategies to build language into everyday routines like mealtime, bath time, and car rides.

Common Questions About Speech

My child isn't talking yet. Is it too early?

Early intervention is key. If you have concerns about your 18-month-old's communication, an evaluation can provide peace of mind or a path forward. We treat children as young as 18 months.

Do you treat stuttering?

Yes. Our SLPs are trained in fluency techniques to help children who stutter speak with confidence and ease.

What if my child needs a device (iPad) to speak?

We specialize in AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication). We can help you navigate the process of obtaining a device, programming it, and training your family on how to use it effectively.
